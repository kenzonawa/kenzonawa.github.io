Problem Statement

The startup ecosystem is very dynamic. Everyday, new companies get founded, others companies get sold, and some even get shutdown for good. It is a fast-paced world where entrepreneurs are always trying to build and develop the next big idea. With that in mind, could there be a trend as to what successful companies go through, and what failing companies do? Would it be possible to gain insight if we could visualize data we have on startups?

Dataset

The dataset being used will be a snapshot of CrunchBase’s 2013 database. CrunchBase is the biggest startup ecosystem database, consisting of investors, incubators and start-ups, which comprises around 500,000 data points profiling companies, people, funds, and fundings. The 2013 database snapshot is freely available online through the following link: https://data.crunchbase.com/docs/2013-snapshot. A Basic Access registration is required.

More specifically, this visualization looks at companies in the tech industry that went through funding and ultimately got sold.

Analysis Task

This visualization aids users to see trends in companies that got funding and how that relates to their acquisition price. Users can get more detailed information with the Parallel Coordinates graph, or simply have a general overview of the companies with RecTransition.

Design Decisions

First and foremost, I wanted users to be able to compare different companies and see the relation between funding and acquisition price. For that, I chose Parallel Coordinates because not only would users be able to compare companies, I would also be able to add each companies specific funding (if they went through Series A,B,C, Seed Investment, or VC).

Once that was done, I noticed it was possible to have somewhat an overview, but information wasn’t really clear. That’s when I decided that I needed some other type of graph that would make it easier for users to see trends in the data. I went to the sketch board and started ideating.

Sketches

I found that RecTransition was a clear and somewhat fun way to see the trends between the companies. You can easily just follow one company as it transitions from its funding value to its acquisition price, or you can follow a specific color to see how companies within that funding range made it.
